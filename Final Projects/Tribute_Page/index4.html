Life of Lola Josefa

A Mother to 7, A Grandma to 13, and A Hero to some

Here's a time line of Lola Josefa's life:

  • 1942 - Born in Sagada,Mountain Province and raised by a single Father
  • 1958 - Graduates from her secondary education in Bangaan National High School
  • 1968 - Marries our Grandfather William Maske Sr, whom she will have 7 kids with.
  • 1983 - During the peak of the NPA (New Peoples Army) everyone's lives back in the province were at danger, as the NPA were recruiting forcefuly.Given that she had 7 kids and no money she had to sell most of her land so she can send her children to the city of Baguio for their own safety.
  • 1984 - Ongoing war between the NPA and the Philippine Army were persistent, and many soldiers (Philippine Army) were seeking refuge on our grandmas house, even though all the other villagers told her not to as it is dangerous and can make her a potential target for the NPA, however, she showed no concern as she still did not hesitate to help those in need as she beleived it was the right thing to do.
  • 1985 - On this year, many soldiers used our grandmas house and fields as a place to seek refuge, and words about it spread all over and surely the NPA heard about it, after that my grandma together with his husband has started receiving different types of threats.
  • 1986 - The following year they all migrated to Baguio and joined her kids who she sent here 3 years ago.
  • 1990 - When they thought life was getting better, her husband--William Maske Sr, was diagnosed with gastric cancer, and the treatement was not cheap, so her kids had to drop out of school and work, and my Grandmother also had to sell pieces of land she had left in the province.
  • 1993 - After 3 hard years of fighting cancer her husband sadly passed away.
  • 1994 - As the wars back in the province was subsiding, and with very little they have, they decided to go back to the province
  • 1999 - Through their hardwork of farming, they were able to buy some portions of their land and her kids were finally able to go to college.
  • 2015 - At the age of 72 Lola Josefa has passed away in her home in Sagada.
